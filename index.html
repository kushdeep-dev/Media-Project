When Art Does
What Facts Cannot

a personal look at art, climate change and why data alone just doesn't cut it

Most people are aware that climate change is an issue, however no one has ever been truly affected by it. That is the question I am attempting to answer.

The Problem

We Know. We Don't Feel.

The public observes climate change due to the widespread reporting on the way oceans rise and forests burn, but the general apathy of people reflects the disconnect between nature and humanity. Most people are aware that climate change is an issue, however no one has ever been truly affected by it.

The perception of climate change contains a sense of distance, while art contains a sense of emotional proximity. Seeing a mural or hearing a song moves you in a way that reading a report never does. I think art may be one of the only forms of communication capable of making people feel for climate change when simply stating the facts will not.

"Traditional scientific methods frequently catalyze psychological denial rather than fostering motivation."

Personal Reflection

What I Actually Think

One of the things I find interesting is how art tries to make people aware about climate change. I've seen all sorts of different examples online, whether it's images or footage from pollution, wildfires or melting ice, and that always seems to stick with me more than just reading about something.

I have always felt like most people are aware that climate change is an issue, however no one has ever been truly affected by it. This is why I believe art may be one of the only forms of communication capable of making people feel for climate change when simply stating the facts will not.

It has been observed that climate change does not affect everyone equally, and art is one of the few mediums that actually tells those stories of inequality. The research poses questions regarding whether art actually changes behavior or just raises awareness, and why seeing a mural or hearing a song moves an individual in a way that reading a report never does.

Take Action

So What Do We Do?

If art is really this powerful then engaging with it and supporting it is itself a form of climate action. Here are three things anyone can do.

01Seek out climate art in your city, murals, installations, exhibitions. Pay attention to what you feel not just what you learn.
02Support artists doing this work. Follow them, share their work, buy prints if you can. Artists cannot create without an audience.
03Make something yourself. A drawing, a poem, a playlist. If it helps you process your feelings about climate change its doing the work.
